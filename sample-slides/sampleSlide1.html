German Energy: Regulatory & Technological Drivers Reshaping Landscape

A

Regulatory Changes

Renewable Energy Act (EEG) established feed-in tariffs guaranteeing 20-year price security for renewable energy producers
Nuclear phase-out law mandated closure of all nuclear plants by 2022, later extended to 2023
Coal phase-out agreement set 2038 deadline for ending coal power generation
Grid expansion laws accelerated transmission infrastructure development to connect renewable sources
B

Technological Changes

Wind power capacity increased from 6 GW to 69 GW, becoming largest renewable source
Solar PV installations grew from 0.1 GW to 81 GW, with costs declining by 85%
Energy storage deployment expanded to 10+ GW capacity to manage grid stability
Smart grid technologies integrated to optimize renewable energy distribution and consumption
Energy prices increased by ~35% since 2000, driving industrial competitiveness concerns
CO₂ emissions from electricity reduced by 50% since 1990 despite nuclear exit
3
Grid stability challenges require €100+ billion infrastructure upgrade by 2030
Source: BMWi, Agora Energiewende, BCG Analysis
